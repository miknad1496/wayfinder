For students, they are a way to stand out when you lack work experience. Many can be started or completed while still in college.\n\n## CFA (Chartered Financial Analyst)\n**Organization**: CFA Institute\n**Field**: Finance & Investment Management\n**Time to complete**: 2.5-5 years typical\n**Cost**: ~$3,000-$5,000 total (registration + exam fees)\n**Requirements**: Bachelor's degree (or final year student can register for Level I), 4,000 hours of relevant work experience for charter\n**Salary impact**: CFA charterholders earn median $180,000+ (varies significantly by role and location)\n**Tip for students**: Start studying in junior/senior year. Pass Level I before or just after graduation to stand out in investment banking, asset management, and equity research roles.\n**Career paths**: Investment Banking, Asset Management, Equity Research, Financial Planning, Risk Management\n\n## CPA (Certified Public Accountant)\n**Organization**: AICPA / State Boards\n**Field**: Accounting\n**Time to complete**: 6-18 months for all sections\n**Cost**: ~$2,000-$4,000 (exam fees + review course)\n**Requirements**: 150 credit hours of education (most states), bachelor's degree, work experience varies by state\n**Salary impact**: CPAs earn 10-15% more than non-CPA accountants. Starting salary: $55,000-$70,000 at Big 4.\n**Tip for students**: The 150 credit hour requirement often means a 5th year or master's. Plan this early. Big 4 firms often prefer candidates who are \"CPA eligible\" at graduation.\n**Career paths**: Public Accounting (Big 4), Corporate Accounting, Tax Advisory, Forensic Accounting, CFO Track\n\n## CompTIA A+\n**Organization**: CompTIA\n**Field**: Information Technology (Entry Level)\n**Time to complete**: 1-3 months of study\n**Cost**: ~$500 (two exam vouchers)\n**Requirements**: No formal prerequisites (recommended: 9-12 months IT experience)\n**Salary impact**: Entry IT roles: $40,000-$55,000. The cert helps get your foot in the door.\n**Tip for students**: Best entry-level IT cert. Earn it during college to qualify for help desk and IT support roles. Pairs well with any major.\n**Career paths**: IT Support Specialist, Help Desk Analyst, Field Service Technician\n\n## CompTIA Security+\n**Organization**: CompTIA\n**Field**: Cybersecurity\n**Time to complete**: 2-4 months of study\n**Cost**: ~$400 (exam voucher)\n**Requirements**: CompTIA A+ or Network+ recommended (not required)\n**Salary impact**: Cybersecurity analyst starting salary: $65,000-$85,000. Required for many DoD positions.\n**Tip for students**: Cybersecurity has a massive talent shortage. This cert plus a bachelor's degree makes you very competitive. Required for government/defense cybersecurity work.\n**Career paths**: Cybersecurity Analyst, Security Engineer, SOC Analyst, Information Security\n\n## AWS Cloud Practitioner\n**Organization**: Amazon Web Services\n**Field**: Cloud Computing\n**Time to complete**: 1-2 months of study\n**Cost**: $100 (exam fee)\n**Requirements**: No prerequisites\n**Salary impact**: AWS-certified professionals earn 25-30% more on average in tech roles\n**Tip for students**: Cloud skills are in massive demand. Start with Cloud Practitioner, then pursue Solutions Architect Associate. AWS offers free training and student credits.\n**Career paths**: Cloud Engineer, DevOps Engineer, Solutions Architect, Cloud Consultant\n\n## Google Data Analytics Certificate\n**Organization**: Google (via Coursera)\n**Field**: Data Analytics\n**Time to complete**: 3-6 months at 10 hrs/week\n**Cost**: ~$200-$300 (Coursera subscription)\n**Requirements**: No prerequisites, no degree required\n**Salary impact**: Data analysts earn median $82,000 (BLS). The cert helps career changers and supplements any major.\n**Tip for students**: This is one of the best bang-for-buck credentials. Teaches SQL, R, Tableau, and data visualization. Pairs excellently with business, social science, or health majors.\n**Career paths**: Data Analyst, Business Intelligence Analyst, Marketing Analyst, Operations Analyst\n\n## CAPM (Certified Associate in Project Management)\n**Organization**: PMI (Project Management Institute)\n**Field**: Project Management\n**Time to complete**: 2-3 months of study\n**Cost**: ~$400 (exam + PMI membership)\n**Requirements**: 23 hours of project management education (free options available)\n**Salary impact**: Project coordinator/analyst starting: $50,000-$65,000. PMP holders earn median $116,000.\n**Tip for students**: Great addition to ANY major. Project management skills are needed everywhere. Earn CAPM in college, then get PMP after 3 years of work experience.\n**Career paths**: Project Coordinator, Business Analyst, Program Manager, Operations Manager\n\n## SHRM-CP\n**Organization**: SHRM (Society for Human Resource Management)\n**Field**: Human Resources\n**Time to complete**: 3-6 months of study\n**Cost**: ~$500 (exam fee, discounted for students)\n**Requirements**: HR-related bachelor's degree or 1 year HR experience\n**Salary impact**: HR specialists with SHRM-CP earn $60,000-$75,000 starting\n**Tip for students**: If you're interested in people-focused business roles, HR is growing and well-compensated.
