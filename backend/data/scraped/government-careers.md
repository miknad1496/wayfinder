  "content": "# Government Career Pathways for Students\n\n*Source: USAJobs.gov, OPM.gov*\n*Updated: 2026-03-17T14:10:03.738Z*\n\n## Student & Recent Graduate Programs\n\n### Pathways Internship Program\n**Eligibility**: Currently enrolled students (high school through graduate school)\n**What it is**: Provides students in high schools, colleges, trade schools, and other qualifying educational institutions with paid work opportunities in federal agencies.\n**Duration**: During enrollment period, may lead to permanent position\n**Pay**: GS-2 to GS-7 depending on education level\n**How to apply**: Search \"Pathways Intern\" on usajobs.gov, must be currently enrolled\n\n### Recent Graduates Program\n**Eligibility**: Graduated within past 2 years (6 years for veterans)\n**What it is**: Full-time positions for recent graduates. Includes formal training, mentoring, and career development.\n**Duration**: 1 year with conversion to permanent position possible\n**Pay**: GS-5 to GS-9 typically\n**How to apply**: Search \"Pathways Recent Graduates\" on usajobs.gov\n\n### Presidential Management Fellows (PMF)\n**Eligibility**: Graduate degree completed within past 2 years\n**What it is**: The most prestigious federal entry program. 2-year fellowship with rotational assignments, training, and mentorship.\n**Duration**: 2 years, converts to permanent GS-12/13\n**Pay**: GS-9 to GS-12\n**How to apply**: Apply at pmf.gov during the annual open season (typically fall)\n\n## Federal Career Basics\n\n### Federal Pay Scale (GS)\nEntry-level college grads typically start at GS-5 ($33,693) or GS-7 ($41,637). With a master's, GS-9 ($50,748). Pay varies by locality. Within 3-5 years, most professionals reach GS-11 ($61,592) or GS-12 ($73,789). Senior positions: GS-13 ($87,758) to GS-15 ($128,078).\n\n### Benefits\nFederal employees receive: health insurance (FEHB — government pays 70-75%), pension (FERS — defined benefit + TSP 401k with 5% match), 13-26 days annual leave per year, 13 sick days, 11 paid holidays, student loan repayment programs (up to $10,000/year), and job security.\n\n### Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF)\nAfter 120 qualifying payments (10 years) while working for the government, remaining federal student loan balance is forgiven. This is a major financial benefit for graduates with high loan balances.\n\n### Security Clearances\nSome positions require clearances (Secret, Top Secret, TS/SCI). Having a clearance significantly increases career options and earning potential, especially in defense and intelligence. Clearance is granted by the employer, not something you apply for independently.\n\n## Visa & Immigration for International Students\n\n### Federal Employment and Citizenship\nMost federal jobs require U.S. citizenship. Some agencies can hire non-citizens for hard-to-fill positions. Check individual job listings for requirements.\n\n### OPT (Optional Practical Training)\nInternational students on F-1 visas get 12 months of work authorization after graduation. STEM majors get an additional 24-month extension (total 36 months). This is the primary path for international students to gain U.S. work experience.\n\n### H-1B Visa\nEmployer-sponsored work visa. Annual cap of 65,000 + 20,000 for U.S. master's holders. Highly competitive lottery system. STEM, healthcare, and tech companies are the most frequent sponsors.\n\n### Industries Most Likely to Sponsor Visas\nTechnology (Google, Amazon, Microsoft, Meta), consulting (Deloitte, McKinsey, BCG), finance (Goldman Sachs, JP Morgan), healthcare systems, and universities. Check myvisajobs.com for company-specific H-1B data.\n\n"
