# Education-Earnings Premium: What Each Degree Level Actually Pays

**Source**: Census ACS 2024 1-Year Estimates
**Created**: March 2026
**Purpose**: Quantify the real dollar value of education credentials for career and education ROI decisions

---

## National Education-Earnings Ladder

| Education Level | Median Earnings | Premium Over HS | Lifetime Gain (40yr) |
|----------------|-----------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Less than High School | $32,468 | -$8,216 (-20%) | -$328,640 |
| High School Diploma | $40,684 | — (baseline) | — |
| Some College / Associate's | $47,953 | +$7,269 (+18%) | +$290,760 |
| Bachelor's Degree | $70,470 | +$29,786 (+73%) | +$1,191,440 |
| Graduate Degree | $90,191 | +$49,507 (+122%) | +$1,980,280 |

**The headline number:** A bachelor's degree holder earns $29,786 more per year than a high school graduate — $1.19 million over a 40-year career before accounting for education costs.

**The nuance:** This is the aggregate median. The actual premium varies enormously by field of study, institution quality, and geographic location. A computer science degree from a top-50 school might deliver 3x this premium. A humanities degree from an expensive private school might deliver 0.5x.

## The Key Decision Points

### High School → Associate's/Some College: The +18% Step
- Annual gain: $7,269
- Cost: $10-30K for community college (2 years)
- Break-even: 1-4 years post-graduation
- **Verdict:** Almost always worth it. Low cost, meaningful earnings bump. Especially strong for healthcare (nursing, dental hygiene), IT, and skilled trades pathways.

### Associate's → Bachelor's: The Big Jump (+47% over associate's)
- Annual gain: $22,517 over associate's ($29,786 over HS)
- Cost: $40-200K+ depending on institution (2 additional years at 4-year school)
- Break-even: 2-9 years depending on cost
- **Verdict:** Worth it IF the total debt stays below Year 1 salary (the Wayfinder Debt Rule). At a state university ($20-40K total cost), the ROI is exceptional. At a private university ($100K+ debt for a non-STEM degree), the ROI is questionable.

### Bachelor's → Graduate Degree: The Diminishing Return
- Annual gain: $19,721 over bachelor's
- Cost: $50-200K+ (2-6 years) plus opportunity cost of lost wages
- Break-even: 3-10+ years
- **Verdict:** Highly field-dependent. Professional degrees (MD, JD, MBA at T15) have strong ROI with caveats. Academic master's degrees (most humanities/social science MA programs) have the weakest ROI of any education investment because the premium is modest and the opportunity cost is real.

## State-by-State Education Premium

The education premium is NOT uniform across states. States with strong knowledge economies pay significantly higher premiums for bachelor's and graduate degrees.

### Highest Bachelor's Degree Premium (over HS, in dollars)
These states reward a 4-year degree the most:
- **District of Columbia**: ~$40K+ annual premium (government, policy, law concentration)
- **Massachusetts, Connecticut, New Jersey, New York**: $30-38K premiums (finance, tech, biotech hubs)
- **California, Washington, Virginia, Maryland**: $28-35K premiums (tech corridors)

### Lowest Bachelor's Degree Premium
- **Mississippi, Arkansas, West Virginia**: $18-22K premiums
- **Montana, South Dakota, Wyoming**: $15-20K premiums
- These states have fewer knowledge-economy jobs, so the degree premium is compressed

**Strategic Insight:** If you're investing $100K+ in a bachelor's degree, your post-graduation location matters as much as the degree itself. A bachelor's degree in DC or Massachusetts pays back nearly twice as fast as the same degree applied in Mississippi — even before adjusting for cost of living.

## The "Is College Worth It?" Framework

When a user asks "is college worth it?", the answer depends on five variables:

1. **What's the total cost?** (Tuition + living + opportunity cost)
2. **What's the target field?** (STEM/business/healthcare → high premium; humanities/education → lower premium)
3. **What's the target location?** (High-premium states → faster payback)
4. **What's the alternative?** (Trades, certifications, and associate's degrees have strong ROI at lower cost)
5. **What's the debt load?** (Apply the Wayfinder Debt Rule: debt must not exceed Year 1 salary)

### Quick ROI Calculator
```
Years to Break Even = Total Education Cost / Annual Earnings Premium
```

Examples:
- State university CS degree: $60K cost / $45K annual premium = **1.3 years** → Excellent
- Private university business degree: $200K cost / $30K annual premium = **6.7 years** → Acceptable
- Private university liberal arts degree: $200K cost / $20K annual premium = **10 years** → Poor
- Community college nursing (RN): $15K cost / $15K annual premium = **1 year** → Outstanding

## Education Premium by Age (Experience Proxy)

The education premium grows with experience. Early-career premiums are smaller because bachelor's degree holders start at entry level. By mid-career, the gap widens dramatically:

| Age Bracket | HS Median | Bachelor's Median | Gap |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------|-----|
| 25-34 | ~$37,000 | ~$55,000 | +$18,000 |
| 35-44 | ~$45,000 | ~$75,000 | +$30,000 |
| 45-54 | ~$48,000 | ~$82,000 | +$34,000 |
| 55-64 | ~$45,000 | ~$78,000 | +$33,000 |

**Critical Insight:** The education premium peaks in the 45-54 bracket. This is when degree holders hit senior/leadership positions while non-degree holders plateau. The argument "I'll catch up without a degree" is statistically unlikely after age 35.

## Integration with Other Wayfinder Data

### Census + BLS: "What should I study?"
Cross-reference Census education premiums with BLS occupation wages to find the highest-ROI education paths. Example: BLS shows Information Security Analysts at $102,600 median with 33% growth. Census shows the bachelor's premium in tech states is $35K+. Combined insight: a cybersecurity bachelor's in a tech state has among the highest education ROIs available.

### Census + COL: "Where should I use my degree?"
A bachelor's degree in DC earns a $40K premium, but DC's RPP is 109.9. Adjusted premium: ~$36K real. A bachelor's in Texas earns a $28K premium, but Texas RPP is 96.5. Adjusted premium: ~$29K real. The nominal gap ($40K vs $28K) shrinks to $7K in real terms — making Texas a surprisingly competitive destination for degree ROI.

### Census + Scorecard: "Which program at which school?"
Census gives the baseline education premium. Scorecard gives program-specific earnings at specific schools. Compare: "Does this specific program at this specific school beat the Census baseline for this degree level?" If a graduate program's alumni earn below the Census graduate median of $90,191, it's underperforming — regardless of the school's reputation.
