Extension of the Top 7 Institutional DNA brain. Same framework: Hidden Ask, Red-Flag Filter, Campus-Specific Easter Eggs. These 10 schools complete coverage of the T20 elite applicant market.

**Cycle Note:** Prompt data sourced from verified 2025-2026 cycle unless noted. Verify exact prompts each cycle.

## 1. UNIVERSITY OF PENNSYLVANIA (UPenn)

### The Hidden Ask

Penn selects for **interdisciplinary pragmatists who build across boundaries**. Penn's "One University" policy — where students can take classes across all four undergraduate schools (College, Wharton, Engineering, Nursing) — is the institutional DNA. They want students who will actually USE this. The hidden question: "Will you combine things that other schools keep separate?"

The **thank-you note prompt** (150-200 words) is Penn's most unusual filter. It tests emotional intelligence and gratitude — not as a soft skill, but as a leadership indicator. Penn reads this for: Who do you notice? Who do you credit? Can you be specific about impact?

**What Penn selects for that others don't:** Cross-school engagement. An applicant to Wharton who mentions taking a class in the College of Arts and Sciences is playing Penn's game correctly. An applicant to the College who never mentions Penn's professional schools is missing the point.

### The Red-Flag Filter

| Cliché | Why It Fails | Wayfinder Alternative |
|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| "Wharton's prestige" | Self-serving. Penn hears this constantly from Wharton applicants | What specific Wharton resource (concentration, club, faculty) fills a specific gap in YOUR trajectory? |
| "Penn's location in Philadelphia" | Geographic fact | Name a specific Philly institution, community, or industry you'd engage with through Penn partnerships |
| "Interdisciplinary opportunities" without specifics | Every school claims this | Name the exact cross-school course combination or dual-degree path you'd pursue |
| Thank-you note that reads as performative | Penn's AOs read 5,000 of these. Generic gratitude fails | Thank someone unexpected. Be surgically specific about WHAT they did and HOW it changed you |

### Campus-Specific Easter Eggs

- **Vagelos Integrated Program in Energy Research (VIPER)**: Dual-degree in science + engineering. Extremely niche, extremely high-signal.
- **Huntsman Program**: International studies + business (College + Wharton dual degree). Name the target language + region.
- **PennApps**: One of the oldest college hackathons. Strong signal for CS applicants.
- **Civic Scholars Program**: For students committed to public service through Penn's Netter Center. References this for social-impact applicants.
- **The Quad vs. College Houses**: Penn's residential system. Naming a specific College House shows you understand Penn's social architecture beyond "campus life."

### Key Supplemental Prompts (Verified 2025-2026)

- **Thank-You Note** (150-200 words): "Write a short thank-you note to someone you have not yet thanked and would like to acknowledge." — This is Penn's most unusual filter. Tests emotional intelligence and specificity. Thank someone unexpected; be surgical about WHAT they did and HOW it changed you.
- **Why Penn / School-Specific** (150-200 words): Standard "Why Penn" essay PLUS a separate prompt tailored to your specific undergraduate school (College, Wharton, Engineering, Nursing). The school-specific essay is where cross-school engagement must appear.
- **Coordinated Dual-Degree Applicants**: Additional prompts required for programs like Huntsman, VIPER, or LSM.

## 2. CALTECH

### The Hidden Ask

Caltech selects for **deep scientific obsession in a tiny, intense community**. Caltech has ~1,000 undergrads. This is not a university — it's a research monastery. The hidden ask: "Will you thrive when surrounded by 999 people who are all as intense as you? Will the absence of a 'normal college experience' energize you rather than isolate you?"

**What Caltech selects for that others don't:** Genuine mathematical or scientific creativity. Not "I got 5s on every AP science exam." They want evidence that you've explored a problem NO ONE ASSIGNED. Caltech doesn't care about leadership, community service, or well-roundedness — they care about intellectual depth at a level that makes MIT look broad.

### The Red-Flag Filter

| Cliché | Why It Fails | Wayfinder Alternative |
|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| "I want to be well-rounded" | Caltech explicitly does NOT want well-rounded. They want spiky | Show one deep obsession pursued to an unusual level |
| "World-class research opportunities" | Every STEM school says this | Name a specific Caltech research group, a specific JPL project, or a specific professor's recent paper |
| "Small class sizes" | True but generic | How does Caltech's 3:1 student-faculty ratio change YOUR specific learning? What would you do with that access? |
| Leadership/service emphasis | Caltech deprioritizes this relative to peers | Mention only if genuinely organic to who you are. Don't force it. |

### Campus-Specific Easter Eggs

- **SURF (Summer Undergraduate Research Fellowships)**: 10-week funded research. Nearly every Caltech student participates. Name the specific lab you'd target.
- **JPL (Jet Propulsion Laboratory)**: Caltech manages JPL for NASA. For space/planetary science applicants, this is THE draw. Be specific about a mission or project.
- **Honor Code culture**: Caltech students take unproctored exams. The Honor Code isn't a rule — it's the social contract. Reference it if integrity is central to your identity.
- **House System**: Caltech's 8 residential houses function like MIT's living groups — distinct cultures, self-governance. Naming a House shows depth.
- **Rotation**: First-year process of visiting all Houses before choosing one. Shows you understand Caltech's social onboarding.

### Key Supplemental Prompts (Verified 2025-2026)

- **Area of Interest** (100-200 words): "If you had to choose an area of interest or two today, what would you choose? Why did you choose your proposed area of interest?" — Caltech wants specificity. Name the subfield, not just "physics." Reference a specific problem or question.
- **Short-Answer Pick-Two** (250 words total across both): Choose 2 of 4 options covering: a hobby that brings joy, a class you would teach, a core piece of your identity, or a concept that blew your mind. The "concept that blew your mind" option is the highest-signal choice for demonstrating intellectual depth.
- **Six supplemental essays total**, heavily STEM-focused. Optional prompt available for extenuating circumstances.

## 3. DUKE UNIVERSITY

### The Hidden Ask

Duke selects for **purposeful engagement with both the intellectual and the experiential**. Duke's culture is unique among elite schools: it combines Ivy-level academics with a major sports culture (basketball is religion), a Southern social scene, and a strong service orientation. The hidden ask: "Are you the kind of person who will do the research AND show up to Cameron Indoor Stadium?"

**What Duke selects for that others don't:** The Trinity/Pratt distinction matters. Duke explicitly asks whether you're applying to Trinity (Arts & Sciences) or Pratt (Engineering). The "Why Duke" essay must reference the correct school's resources. Mentioning a Pratt lab in a Trinity application (or vice versa) is an instant credibility hit.

### The Red-Flag Filter

| Cliché | Why It Fails | Wayfinder Alternative |
|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| "Duke's basketball culture" as a selling point | Every applicant knows about Duke basketball | If sports matter, reference the COMMUNITY around Cameron — the culture of shared intensity — not the sport itself |
| "Durham's growing tech scene" | Generic economic boosterism | Name a specific Duke partnership (Duke Innovation & Entrepreneurship, or a specific RTP connection) |
| "Beautiful campus" | Tourist language | What about Duke's physical environment enables YOUR specific work? (Duke Forest for ecology, the Nasher Museum for art history) |
| "Strong academics and social life" | Describes every school | What specific COMBINATION at Duke doesn't exist elsewhere? |

### Campus-Specific Easter Eggs

- **Bass Connections**: Interdisciplinary research teams of faculty, grad students, and undergrads working on real problems. Extremely high-signal.
- **DukeEngage**: Fully-funded summer immersive service program. For service-oriented applicants, name the specific DukeEngage program + location.
- **Focus Program**: First-year interdisciplinary living-learning clusters. Naming a specific Focus cluster shows deep research.
- **Duke Forest**: 7,000-acre research forest. Unique to Duke. Environmental science applicants should know this exists.
- **Pratt's First-Year Design Experience**: All Pratt freshmen take a hands-on design course. Engineering applicants should reference this.

### Key Supplemental Prompts (Verified 2025-2026)

- **Why Duke** (250 words max): "What is your impression of Duke as a university and community, and why do you believe it is a good match for your goals, values, and interests? If there is something specific that attracts you to our academic offerings... feel free to include that too." — The Trinity/Pratt distinction MUST appear here. Every sentence should be specific to the correct school.
- **Two optional prompts** also available. The required essay is where fit evidence lives — don't leave the optional prompts blank if you have genuine material.

## 4. JOHNS HOPKINS UNIVERSITY

### The Hidden Ask

Hopkins selects for **research-driven doers who start before they're told to**. The sesquicentennial "first step" essay prompt is not an accident — it reveals Hopkins's DNA. They value INITIATIVE over polish, BEGINNING over finishing. The hidden ask: "Have you started something meaningful before anyone gave you permission?"

**What Hopkins selects for that others don't:** Research intensity at the undergraduate level. Hopkins is one of the few schools where undergraduates publish in peer-reviewed journals at meaningful rates. If you don't mention research interest in your Hopkins application, you're misreading the room.

### The Red-Flag Filter

| Cliché | Why It Fails | Wayfinder Alternative |
|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| "Hopkins is a great pre-med school" | Reductive. Hopkins is a research university, not a pre-med pipeline | Frame your interest in terms of RESEARCH, even if your goal is medicine. Hopkins wants MD-researchers, not MD-clinicians-only |
| "Baltimore's challenges" as a generic diversity reference | Condescending if not backed by specific engagement | If you reference Baltimore, name a specific community partnership, health initiative, or neighborhood program |
| "Top-ranked in many fields" | Ranking citation is not personal insight | Which SPECIFIC Hopkins program, center, or lab aligns with your research question? |
| The "first step" essay about a trivial first | Missing the prompt's intent | The first step should be genuinely formative — not "the first time I tried sushi" |

### Campus-Specific Easter Eggs

- **Woodrow Wilson Undergraduate Research Fellowship**: Funded independent research for undergrads. Name this.
- **Bloomberg School of Public Health**: World's #1 public health school, and undergrads can cross-register. ENORMOUS draw for health-adjacent applicants.
- **Applied Physics Laboratory (APL)**: Like Caltech's JPL relationship. Hopkins manages APL for the Department of Defense.
- **Peabody Institute**: Hopkins's conservatory. For musician-scientists, the ability to study at both Peabody and the Krieger School is unique.
- **The Johns Hopkins Science Review show**: Historical reference showing Hopkins's long commitment to public science communication. Deep-cut culture signal.

### Key Supplemental Prompts (Verified 2025-2026)

- **Sesquicentennial "First Step"** (350 words): "Over the past 150 years, every monumental discovery at Hopkins has started with a first step... Tell us about an important first in your life—big or small—that has shaped you." — The prompt rewards INITIATIVE. The "first" must be genuinely formative — not trivial. Show that you START things before anyone gives you permission. Connect the first to ongoing momentum, not a one-time event.

## 5. NORTHWESTERN UNIVERSITY

### The Hidden Ask

Northwestern selects for **contextual self-awareness and cross-disciplinary versatility**. The required prompt explicitly asks how your background shapes how you'd engage. Northwestern wants students who understand that WHERE they come from affects HOW they think — and can articulate that with specificity.

**What Northwestern selects for that others don't:** The quarter system + proximity to Chicago + cross-school access creates a unique profile. Northwestern wants students who will use Medill (journalism), Bienen (music), and McCormick (engineering) even if they're admitted to Weinberg (arts & sciences). Cross-pollination is the institutional brand.

### The Red-Flag Filter

| Cliché | Why It Fails | Wayfinder Alternative |
|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| "Northwestern's location near Chicago" | Geographic fact shared with 20+ schools | Name what you'd DO in Chicago through Northwestern — a specific internship program, the Chicago Field Studies, or an industry connection |
| "Strong academics and great social life" | Applies to every school | What specific Northwestern intersection (journalism + data science, theater + engineering) matches YOUR interests? |
| "I'm well-rounded" | Northwestern wants T-shaped people (depth + breadth), not circles | Show your depth FIRST, then how Northwestern's cross-school access enables your breadth |
| "Wildcat spirit" | Surface school pride | Reference a specific tradition or community with substance (Dance Marathon, Waa-Mu Show) |

### Campus-Specific Easter Eggs

- **Medill School of Journalism**: One of the top journalism schools in the world. Even non-journalism students reference this as a unique Northwestern resource.
- **Bienen School of Music dual degree**: Study music AND another field simultaneously. Unique at this level.
- **Garage**: Northwestern's student startup incubator. For entrepreneurial applicants, this is the Chicago-area equivalent of Stanford's StartX.
- **Dance Marathon (DM)**: Northwestern's 30-hour dance marathon raises millions for charity. It's the largest student-run philanthropy in the country. Cultural DNA.
- **Chicago Field Studies**: Quarter-long internship/study program in downtown Chicago. Name this for anyone emphasizing professional experience.

### Key Supplemental Prompts (Verified 2025-2026)

- **Background/Context Essay** (300 words max): "What aspects of your background (your identity, your school setting, your community, your household, etc.) have most shaped how you see yourself engaging in Northwestern's community...?" — Northwestern explicitly asks how WHERE you come from shapes HOW you'd engage. This demands contextual self-awareness, not just a list of activities.
- **Optional Essays** (200 words max each, choose up to 2): Includes the famous "Painting 'The Rock'" prompt and a question about collaborating on a business or project. The Rock prompt is a cultural fluency test — if you reference it, show you understand it as a community expression, not just vandalism.

## 6. BROWN UNIVERSITY

### The Hidden Ask

Brown selects for **self-directed intellectual explorers**. Brown's Open Curriculum — no required courses, no distribution requirements — is the defining institutional feature. The hidden ask is severe: "Can you be trusted to design your own education? Do you have enough intellectual motivation that you'll take challenging courses even when no one makes you?"

**What Brown selects for that others don't:** Students who would be FRUSTRATED by a structured curriculum. Brown doesn't want people who need guardrails. They want people who would chafe under Columbia's Core or UChicago's Common Core. If structure is your strength, Brown is the wrong school — and your application will reveal it.

### The Red-Flag Filter

| Cliché | Why It Fails | Wayfinder Alternative |
|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| "The Open Curriculum lets me explore" | Every Brown applicant says this | What SPECIFIC cross-disciplinary path would you create? Name courses from 2-3 departments that form YOUR custom curriculum |
| "No grades" (misunderstanding S/NC) | Brown has grades. The S/NC option is available but not mandatory | Reference S/NC correctly: explain how you'd use the option to take intellectual risks in unfamiliar departments |
| "Collaborative not competitive" | Every school claims this | What specifically about Brown's academic culture (pass-fail experimentation, no GPA) enables collaboration that other schools can't? |
| "Providence is charming" | Tourist language | If mentioning Providence, connect to a specific community engagement (Brown's Swearer Center, a local organization) |

### Campus-Specific Easter Eggs

- **PLME (Program in Liberal Medical Education)**: 8-year combined BA/MD. One of few guaranteed medical school admission programs. ULTRA-competitive.
- **Brown|RISD Dual Degree**: 5-year dual degree with the Rhode Island School of Design. For art + liberal arts students, this is unmatched.
- **Swearer Center for Public Service**: Brown's community engagement hub. Name a specific Swearer program for service-oriented applicants.
- **Group Independent Study Projects (GISPs)**: Students can CREATE their own courses. If you have a specific course you'd design, this is the perfect reference.
- **The Ratty and the Blue Room**: Brown's dining culture. Light-touch cultural reference.

### Key Supplemental Prompts (Verified 2025-2026)

- **Open Curriculum Essays** (~200-250 words each): Multiple short essays typically focused on how the applicant would use Brown's Open Curriculum. The SLM must verify exact prompts each cycle — Brown refreshes these annually. The key strategic move: name specific courses from 2-3 departments that form a custom curriculum path. Generic "I want to explore" language is the #1 failure mode.
- **PLME Applicants** (250 words): "Healthcare is constantly changing as it is affected by racial and social inequities... How will you, as a future physician, make a positive impact?" — This is a values-and-systems question, not a clinical-aspiration question. Frame the answer around structural change, not personal heroism.

## 7. DARTMOUTH COLLEGE

### The Hidden Ask

Dartmouth selects for **outdoors-oriented community builders who thrive in a small, remote setting**. Dartmouth is in Hanover, New Hampshire — rural, cold, and isolated. This is a FEATURE, not a bug, but only for the right student. The hidden ask: "Will you be bored here, or will you create your own world?"

Dartmouth recently reinstated test requirements AND became need-blind for international students (2022). These policy shifts signal institutional confidence and a desire to attract the global best while maintaining its intimate, community-centered culture.

**What Dartmouth selects for that others don't:** The D-Plan (Dartmouth Plan) — a year-round quarter system that includes a sophomore summer on campus and off-terms for study abroad, internships, or independent projects. Students who understand and embrace the D-Plan's flexibility show genuine fit.

### The Red-Flag Filter

| Cliché | Why It Fails | Wayfinder Alternative |
|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| "Small school, close community" | Describes 200+ LACs | What about Dartmouth's SPECIFIC smallness (4,500 undergrads, no graduate school dominating resources) changes your experience? |
| "I love the outdoors" without specifics | Dartmouth is outdoorsy, but this alone isn't an essay | Connect outdoor culture to HOW you think, learn, or create — not just a hobby list |
| "Ivy League quality with a LAC feel" | Marketing-speak | Name the specific program, professor, or structure that creates this dynamic |
| "Greek life" references without nuance | Dartmouth's Greek system is complex and sometimes controversial | Don't avoid it, but don't make it your selling point. Reference it only if genuinely central to your social vision |

### Campus-Specific Easter Eggs

- **The D-Plan**: Quarter system with required sophomore summer residency + flexible off-terms. Reference how you'd structure YOUR 4 years.
- **Dartmouth Outing Club (DOC)**: One of oldest and largest collegiate outing clubs. Runs Freshman Trips — a pre-orientation wilderness experience 95%+ of freshmen attend.
- **Tuck School of Business bridge programs**: Undergrads can access Tuck resources. Business-oriented students should reference this.
- **Dartmouth's indigenous heritage**: Dartmouth was originally chartered (1769) partly to educate Native American students. The Native American Studies program and Native community are significant. Reference respectfully if relevant.
- **WISP (Women in Science Project)**: Mentorship program pairing first-year women with faculty researchers. High-signal for STEM women.

### Key Supplemental Prompts (Verified 2025-2026)

- **Choose-One Prompt** (250 words max): Select from several options, including: "There is a Quaker saying: Let your life speak. Describe the environment in which you were raised and the impact it has had on the person you are today." — Dartmouth's prompt rewards environmental self-awareness. The strongest responses connect the physical/social environment (rural, urban, immigrant household, etc.) to specific intellectual or personal formation. Don't just describe — show causation.

## 8. CORNELL UNIVERSITY

### The Hidden Ask

Cornell selects for **specificity of academic interest within a chosen college**. Cornell has 7 undergraduate colleges, each with its own admissions process. The hidden ask is different from any peer: "Do you know WHICH Cornell you're applying to, and can you explain why THAT college specifically?"

**What Cornell selects for that others don't:** Cornell is the most pre-professional of the Ivies. Students apply to a specific college (Arts & Sciences, Engineering, Agriculture & Life Sciences, Hotel Administration, ILR, Human Ecology, Architecture). The "Why Cornell" is really "Why this college at Cornell." A student who writes about Cornell generically without engaging their specific college's identity has functionally written a non-answer.

Cornell recently returned to test-required (Fall 2026+). This signals that stats matter more here than at some test-optional peers.

### The Red-Flag Filter

| Cliché | Why It Fails | Wayfinder Alternative |
|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| "Cornell's Ivy League status with a public mission" | True but generic | Explain how the specific college you chose (CAS? ILR? ALS?) reflects YOUR values and career path |
| "Beautiful gorges and campus" | Every applicant who visits says this | Don't mention the gorges. Or if you must, connect to a specific academic program (geology, ecology) |
| "Diversity of programs" | This describes a university catalog, not personal fit | Name the specific major + specific course sequence within YOUR college |
| Writing a "Why Cornell" that could apply to any of the 7 colleges | Proves you don't understand Cornell's structure | Every sentence should be specific to your chosen college |

### Campus-Specific Easter Eggs

- **Cornell Hotel Administration (SHA)**: The #1 hospitality program globally. If this is your college, reference a specific Statler Hotel experience or industry practicum.
- **ILR School (Industrial and Labor Relations)**: One-of-a-kind undergraduate program. For labor law, HR, or policy-oriented students, this is unmatched.
- **Cornell AgriTech at Geneva**: Research station for food science and agriculture. Niche but powerful for ALS applicants.
- **Pre-Enroll system**: Cornell's course selection process is college-specific. Knowing this shows you understand how academics work at Cornell.
- **Project Teams**: 30+ student-led engineering teams (rocketry, autonomous vehicles, etc.). Name a specific team for engineering applicants.

### Key Supplemental Prompts (Verified 2025-2026)

- **Community Essay** (350 words max): "We all contribute to, and are influenced by, the communities that are meaningful to us. Share how you've been shaped by one of the communities you belong to... Define community in the way that is most meaningful to you." — The trap: picking a community that's too broad (e.g., "my school") or too generic. The winning move: define community narrowly and specifically, then show reciprocal influence (how it shaped you AND how you shaped it).
- **College-Specific Essay**: Word counts and prompts vary by school. Agriculture & Life Sciences requires a 500-word "Why this major?" essay. Each of Cornell's 7 colleges has distinct supplemental requirements — the SLM MUST ensure the student is answering the correct college's prompts.

## 9. RICE UNIVERSITY

### The Hidden Ask

Rice selects for **collaborative intellectuals who value community over competition in a uniquely Southern context**. Rice is small (4,000 undergrads), has a residential college system, is in Houston (the 4th-largest US city), and costs significantly less than peers. The hidden ask: "Do you value community and accessibility, or are you just applying because it's a T20?"

**What Rice selects for that others don't:** The residential college system is NOT like Harvard Houses or Yale Colleges — at Rice, the residential college IS your social life, your intramural team, your study group, and your identity for 4 years. Rice cares deeply about whether you'll be a good college citizen.

### The Red-Flag Filter

| Cliché | Why It Fails | Wayfinder Alternative |
|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| "Rice is a hidden gem" | Condescending. Rice knows its own ranking | What SPECIFIC Rice resource matches your SPECIFIC goal? |
| "Houston's diversity" | True but generic | Name a specific Houston institution (Texas Medical Center, NASA Johnson Space Center, Energy Corridor) you'd engage with through Rice |
| "Small school, big city" | Describes multiple schools | How does Rice's specific relationship with Houston (partnerships, internships, cultural access) serve YOUR trajectory? |
| "Affordable compared to Ivies" | Don't mention cost as a reason to attend | If financial accessibility matters to your story, frame it as Rice's VALUES aligning with yours |

### Campus-Specific Easter Eggs

- **Residential Colleges**: 11 colleges, each with distinct personality. Naming one you'd gravitate toward shows deep research.
- **O-Week**: Rice's orientation week is legendary — run entirely by student advisors within each residential college. Cultural DNA.
- **Texas Medical Center proximity**: The world's largest medical center is across the street from Rice. For pre-med or biomedical applicants, this is unmatched access.
- **Beer Bike**: Annual inter-college bike race + beer relay. Rice's biggest tradition. Light cultural reference.
- **RISE (Rice Initiative for STEM Education)**: Education outreach. For teaching-oriented STEM students.

### Key Supplemental Prompts (Verified 2025-2026)

- **Residential College Essay** (500 words, choose 1 of 2): "The Residential College System is at the heart of Rice student life... What life experiences and/or unique perspectives are you looking forward to sharing with fellow Owls in the residential college system?" — Rice's residential college IS the social architecture. The prompt tests whether you understand that. Don't just say "I'm collaborative" — name a specific perspective, experience, or skill you'd bring to college life.
- **Two Additional Short Essays** (150 words max each): Required. These test concision and personality.
- **Image Upload**: Required — an image that tells Rice something about you. This is a creativity and personality filter, not an art test.

## 10. UNIVERSITY OF NOTRE DAME

### The Hidden Ask

Notre Dame selects for **mission-driven students with a service orientation rooted in (but not limited to) Catholic identity**. Notre Dame's Catholic character is not decorative — it's structural. The hidden ask: "Can you engage seriously with questions of purpose, meaning, and service — whether or not you're Catholic?"

**What Notre Dame selects for that others don't:** Values-driven motivation. At most T20 schools, "I want to make money in finance" is an acceptable career goal. At Notre Dame, that answer needs a moral framing — WHY finance, in SERVICE of what? Notre Dame selects for people who think about their careers as vocations, not just trajectories.

Notre Dame is need-blind for both domestic AND international students and commits to meeting 100% of demonstrated need. This is a significant financial signal.

### The Red-Flag Filter

| Cliché | Why It Fails | Wayfinder Alternative |
|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| "Notre Dame's Catholic values align with mine" | Too easy. What SPECIFICALLY about the Catholic intellectual tradition attracts you? | Reference a specific aspect — the emphasis on social justice, the philosophical tradition, the service ethic — and connect it to YOUR experience |
| "Football culture" | Every applicant knows about Notre Dame football | If mentioning athletics, connect to the COMMUNITY experience, not the sport itself |
| "Strong alumni network" | True of every elite school | Name a specific Notre Dame alumni connection, initiative, or mentoring program |
| Ignoring the Catholic identity entirely | Reads as not understanding the school | You don't need to BE Catholic, but you need to engage with what the Catholic mission MEANS for intellectual life |

### Campus-Specific Easter Eggs

- **The First Year of Studies**: Every freshman enters the same program before declaring a major. Reference how you'd use this exploration year.
- **Center for Social Concerns (CSC)**: Service-learning hub. 80%+ of students participate. Name a specific CSC program or immersion trip.
- **The Hesburgh Libraries and "Touchdown Jesus"**: Cultural touchstone. Light reference for cultural fluency.
- **CUSE (Center for Undergraduate Scholarly Engagement)**: Undergraduate research office. For research-oriented applicants.
- **Notre Dame's London, Dublin, and Rome Global Gateways**: Study abroad programs are heavily utilized. Name a specific Gateway if study abroad is part of your plan.

### Key Supplemental Prompts (Verified 2025-2026)

- **College Search Non-Negotiable** (150 words max): "Tell us about your 'non-negotiable' factor(s) when searching for your future college home." — The strategic play: name a non-negotiable that Notre Dame uniquely satisfies. If your non-negotiable is "strong academics" — that's every school. If it's "an institution where questions of purpose and vocation are taken seriously in the curriculum" — that's Notre Dame.
- **Pick-Three Short Answers** (50-100 words each): Choose 3 from options including: how faith influences your decisions, distinctive personal experiences, fostering service to others, and a compliment you're most proud of receiving. The faith question is NOT a trap — you don't need to be Catholic, but you need to engage authentically. The service question aligns with Notre Dame's CSC culture. The "compliment" question tests self-awareness and humility.
