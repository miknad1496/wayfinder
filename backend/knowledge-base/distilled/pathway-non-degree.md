# Non-Degree Career Paths That Actually Pay: A Reality Check

**Document Purpose**: Career intelligence for students evaluating paths that bypass the 4-year degree and still reach $60K+ within 5 years.

**Key Premise**: Degree-free careers exist at scale, pay well, offer job security, and build real wealth without debt. But not all are created equal. This document separates hype from reality.

---

## Executive Summary

The non-degree career landscape has shifted dramatically in the past 5 years. Skilled trades are experiencing genuine labor shortages with $60K-$100K+ earnings. Tech certifications create accelerated pathways when paired with strategic job placement. Government careers offer a "wealth-building hack" few students know about: entry-level positions that fund your future, come with lifetime pensions, and employ federal student loan forgiveness.

**The uncomfortable truth**: "Learn to code in 12 weeks and make $120K" is a marketing claim, not a career path. But $65K-$85K cybersecurity roles for people holding a Security+ cert? That's real. Electricians routinely clearing $75K-$95K without college debt? That's real.

This document identifies which paths are legitimately accessible and which require navigating a harder climb than the marketing suggests.

## Part 1: Skilled Trades (The Underrated Path)

### Why This Matters Now

Skilled trades currently face a 10-15 year worker deficit. Boomers retiring faster than Gen Z enters the field. Average tradesperson reaches $60K by year 4-5, often without additional education costs beyond apprenticeship.

### Electrician (Licensed)

**The Role**: Install, maintain, repair electrical systems in residential, commercial, industrial settings. Union electricians organize work. Non-union electricians work for contractors or self-employ.

**Training Path**:
- Apprenticeship: 5,000 hours on-the-job + 900 hours classroom (3-5 years)
- Cost: Often FREE through union programs or paid-while-training. Non-union apprenticeships may cost $3K-$8K
- Timeline: 4-5 years to full licensure (journeyman)
- Certifications: State licensing exam required (varies by state; $300-$500 exam fees)

**Year-1 Salary (Post-Apprenticeship)**:
- HCOL (NYC, SF, Boston): $65K-$75K (union scale)
- MCOL (Austin, Denver, Atlanta): $55K-$65K
- LCOL (rural areas): $45K-$55K

**Year-5 Salary**:
- HCOL: $95K-$130K+ (master electrician or self-employed)
- MCOL: $75K-$95K
- LCOL: $60K-$75K

**Career Ceiling Without Degree**: $150K+ (especially true for union electricians, service specialization like EV charging, or self-employment). No ceiling in practical sense—many electricians run six-figure firms.

**Ceiling-Breaking Upgrades**:
- Become master electrician (additional testing, usually 2-5 years experience requirement)
- Specialize in high-demand areas: EV charging infrastructure, solar installation, industrial controls
- Launch contracting firm (capital required: $20K-$100K)

**Demand Trajectory**: Growing. EV infrastructure boom, aging building stock requiring rewiring, data center expansion. AI/automation risk: LOW (houses and buildings still need humans to install wiring).

**The Hidden Version**: EV Charging Infrastructure Electrician. Most electricians don't specialize here yet. It pays 15-25% more than standard electrical work, and demand is exploding (Biden infrastructure bill, corporate EV commitments). Requires 1-2 years additional training in EV systems.

**Insider Notes**:
- Union vs. non-union matters. Union electricians in major metros make $70K-$120K+. Non-union is more variable but faster to earning potential.
- Residential electricians start faster ($50K-$60K in year 1-2) but plateau lower. Commercial/industrial takes longer to reach but hits $100K+.
- Self-employment after mastery is viable: one solo electrician in a midsize market can run $200K-$400K annual revenue business.

### Plumber (Licensed)

**The Role**: Install, repair, maintain water, gas, and waste systems. Residential and commercial. Union and non-union.

**Training Path**:
- Apprenticeship: 5,000 on-the-job hours + 900 classroom hours (typically 4-5 years)
- Cost: Union apprenticeships typically free. Non-union may be $2K-$6K
- Timeline: 4-5 years to journeyman license
- Certifications: State licensing exam ($250-$400)

**Year-1 Salary (Post-Apprenticeship)**:
- HCOL: $60K-$70K
- MCOL: $50K-$60K
- LCOL: $40K-$50K

**Year-5 Salary**:
- HCOL: $90K-$120K+
- MCOL: $70K-$90K
- LCOL: $55K-$70K

**Career Ceiling**: $120K-$180K+ especially via self-employment. Emergency calls command premium pricing. High-end residential (luxury renovations) pays substantially more.

**Upgrade Paths**: Master plumber certification, specialization in gas line installation (higher risk = higher pay), water heater/boiler specialty, launch plumbing company.

**Demand Trajectory**: Growing. Aging housing stock, new construction, pandemic home renovation wave has normalized the market. AI/automation risk: VERY LOW (pipes are physical, require human judgment and adaptation).

**The Hidden Version**: Backflow Prevention Specialist. Most plumbers don't get this certification (requires additional 40-80 hours). Commercial buildings legally need certified backflow inspectors annually. One person can run an inspection service: $200-$400 per inspection, 5-10 per day = $1K-$4K revenue per day. This pays 30-50% more than standard plumbing.

**Insider Notes**:
- Plumbing is messier, more variable hours, more on-call stress than electrical
- Emergency calls (nights/weekends) provide substantial premium pay, key to reaching $100K+
- Geographic variation is large: plumbers in legacy unions (NYC, SF) earn significantly more
- Self-employment trajectory is strong; many plumbers transition to owning shops

### HVAC Technician (EPA-Certified)

**The Role**: Install, maintain, repair heating, cooling, ventilation systems. Residential, commercial, industrial.

**Training Path**:
- Apprenticeship or technical school: 900-1,800 classroom hours + 4,500-6,000 on-the-job hours (3-5 years)
- Cost: Apprenticeships free or paid-while-training. Trade schools: $8K-$18K
- Timeline: 3-5 years
- Certifications: EPA Section 608 Certification (handling refrigerants) is REQUIRED ($250-$400 exam; 1-2 weeks study). Optional journeyman license varies by state.

**Year-1 Salary**:
- HCOL: $55K-$70K
- MCOL: $45K-$60K
- LCOL: $35K-$50K

**Year-5 Salary**:
- HCOL: $85K-$110K
- MCOL: $65K-$85K
- LCOL: $50K-$70K

**Career Ceiling**: $100K-$150K+ (master technician, specialty work, self-employment).

**Upgrade Paths**: Specialization in commercial/industrial (higher pay than residential), EPA certification as trainer/inspector, launch HVAC firm.

**Demand Trajectory**: Strong. Climate change driving AC demand. Heat pump installation boom (Biden climate goals). Aging systems needing replacement. AI/automation risk: VERY LOW.

**The Hidden Version**: Heat Pump Installation Specialist during the transition. Federal/state incentives are making heat pump installation explode. Contractors need experienced installers yesterday. Premium pay right now (2026): 20-40% more than standard HVAC. This is time-limited (5-10 year window likely) but currently the highest-paying version of HVAC work.

**Insider Notes**:
- Residential HVAC is steady but seasonal (intense in summer/winter, slow in spring/fall)
- Commercial HVAC is more stable year-round and pays 15-25% more
- Maintenance contracts provide recurring revenue; key to high earning
- Specializing in diagnostics/troubleshooting of complex systems pays significantly more than basic installations

## Part 2: Tech Certifications (The Speed Run)

### CompTIA A+ → IT Support Role

**The Role**: Help desk, IT technician, desktop support. Troubleshoot hardware, software, networks. Entry-level IT position.

**Training Path**:
- Self-study: 60-80 hours of study (1-3 months)
- Cost: $500 exam fee (two tests), $200-$500 study materials
- Timeline: 1-3 months
- Certifications: CompTIA A+ (220-1101 and 220-1102)

**Year-1 Salary**:
- HCOL: $45K-$55K
- MCOL: $40K-$48K
- LCOL: $35K-$43K

**Year-5 Salary**:
- HCOL: $65K-$80K (if upgraded to network/security cert or promoted to senior role)
- MCOL: $55K-$70K
- LCOL: $45K-$60K

**Career Ceiling Without Upgrade**: $65K-$75K (senior technician roles). To exceed this, need additional certifications (Network+, Security+) or pivot to specialized IT.

**Upgrade Paths**: CompTIA Network+ ($500, 3 months) → Network Admin ($70K-$90K). Then Security+ ($400, 3 months) → Security roles ($80K-$130K+).

**Demand Trajectory**: Stable. Growing remote IT support roles. AI/automation risk: MODERATE (many support tasks automatable, but complex troubleshooting still human). Ceiling likely to narrow over 10 years.

**The Hidden Version**: IT support specialist for healthcare organizations. Healthcare IT support pays 15-20% more than general enterprise IT, offers better benefits, more stable hours. Requires knowledge of HIPAA compliance and healthcare systems, not a separate cert—just specialized domain knowledge.

**Realistic Assessment**: A+ alone is not a $60K+ path to year-5. It's a foundation. You need at least two additional certifications (Network+ and Security+) or lateral movement to specializations to exceed $60K by year 5. Timeline: A+ (3 months) → Network+ (3 months, working in help desk) → Security+ (3 months, still working) = 9 months of study while employed. Realistic year-5 with this sequence: $75K-$90K in HCOL markets.

### CompTIA Security+ → Cybersecurity Role

**The Role**: Security analyst, junior cybersecurity engineer, threat analyst. Monitor networks for breaches, respond to incidents, manage firewalls.

**Training Path**:
- Prerequisites: CompTIA Network+ (220-1101) strongly recommended first ($500, 3 months)
- Study: 60-80 hours (2-4 months)
- Cost: $400-$500 exam fee, $200-$400 study materials
- Timeline: 5-7 months total (Network+ then Security+)
- Certifications: CompTIA Security+

**Year-1 Salary (With Certs)**:
- HCOL: $65K-$80K
- MCOL: $55K-$70K
- LCOL: $45K-$60K

**Year-5 Salary**:
- HCOL: $95K-$130K
- MCOL: $80K-$110K
- LCOL: $70K-$95K

**Career Ceiling Without Degree**: $130K-$180K+ (senior security analyst, manager roles). Security+ is widely respected; many reach manager/architect roles without degrees.

**Upgrade Paths**: CISSP ($750, requires 5 years security experience) → senior roles $150K+. Cloud security certs (AWS Security Specialty) add 15-25% premium. DoD Secret clearance requirement drives salaries up significantly.

**Demand Trajectory**: Growing rapidly. Cyber threats accelerating. Regulatory requirements increasing. AI/automation risk: LOW (threat detection increasingly AI-assisted, but response and strategy remain human). Shortage of qualified personnel.

**The Hidden Version**: DoD Contractor Security Analyst. Security+ is required for DoD Secret clearance jobs. These roles are often 15-25% higher pay than commercial equivalents AND offer remote work, stability, excellent benefits. The barrier: background investigation (takes 3-6 months), but once passed, you're in a protected labor market. A typical DoD contractor Security+ role: $85K-$110K in MCOL, with outstanding benefits.

**Real Numbers Check**: Reddit career threads from 2024-2025 confirm: Security+ holders getting $70K-$95K offers in major metros after 6-12 months work experience. With some prior IT background, $85K-$110K is realistic by year 3-5.

**Year-1 Salary (Cloud Practitioner Only)**:
- HCOL: $55K-$70K

- HCOL: $100K-$135K

**Salary Premium**: Data shows AWS cert holders earn 25-30% more than non-certified peers in same roles.

**Upgrade Paths**: AWS Solutions Architect Professional ($300) → $150K-$200K. Multi-cloud credentials (AWS + Azure + GCP) → top tier roles. Kubernetes (CKAD, CKA) adds specialization premium.

**Demand Trajectory**: Explosive growth. Cloud adoption accelerating. Shortage of senior cloud engineers. AI/automation risk: LOW for next 5-10 years (infrastructure architecture is complex, long-term decisions are human-driven).

**The Hidden Version**: Kubernetes Cluster Administrator (CKAD cert, $395). Kubernetes is the container orchestration standard. Most cloud engineers aren't also Kubernetes certified. Adds 15-20% salary premium and significantly increases job options. Combined AWS + Kubernetes path: $130K-$180K by year 5.

**Real Data**: LinkedIn salary data (2025) shows AWS Certified Solutions Architects at $120K-$160K median in major metros. Growth trajectory is steep; year 1-2 roles often in $65K-$85K range, then jump to $110K-$140K by year 3-5.

## Part 3: Healthcare Non-Degree Paths

### Registered Nursing (Associate Degree Path)

**The Role**: Nursing care, patient assessment, medication administration, clinical judgment.

**Training Path**:
- Associate Degree in Nursing (ADN): 2 years full-time (community college)
- Cost: $10K-$25K total tuition
- Timeline: 2 years
- Certifications: NCLEX-RN licensing exam ($200)

**Year-1 Salary (Post-License)**:
- HCOL: $60K-$75K
- MCOL: $52K-$65K
- LCOL: $45K-$58K

**Year-5 Salary**:
- HCOL: $85K-$110K
- MCOL: $72K-$95K
- LCOL: $60K-$80K

**Career Ceiling Without Further Degree**: $80K-$100K (senior nurse, charge nurse). To exceed $100K, typically need Bachelor's (BSN) or specialization.

**Upgrade Paths**: RN to BSN (1-2 years, tuition $5K-$15K, often while working) → certified specialties (ICU, ER, critical care) earn 15-20% premium. Nurse practitioner (4 more years, masters) → $120K-$160K.

**Demand Trajectory**: Growing significantly. Nursing shortage accelerating. Burnout concerns but no slowdown in job availability. AI/automation risk: VERY LOW (clinical judgment and patient care require humans).

**The Hidden Version**: Travel Nurse. Temporary assignments (8-13 weeks) in understaffed hospitals. Base pay equivalent, but adds $500-$1,500/week housing stipend + meal allowance + equipment stipend. Effective total: $90K-$130K annually with travel assignments. Many nurses combine home base (stability) with periodic travel assignments (income boost).

**Insider Notes**:
- ADN vs. BSN: ADN is faster to career ($2 years vs 4), but BSN is increasingly preferred. Most ADN nurses pursue BSN within 5-10 years. Plan $5K-$15K additional cost.
- Shift differentials matter: night shift, weekend shift add 10-15% to base pay
- Union nursing offers better pay/benefits (10-20% premium in many regions)
- Specializations (ER, ICU, OR) pay 10-20% more but have steep learning curves

## Part 4: Government Careers (The Wealth-Building Hack)

### Federal Pathways Internship + Conversion

**The Role**: Administrative/analytical positions, GS-2 to GS-7 (Federal salary scale).

**Training Path**:
- Eligibility: U.S. Citizen, pass background check
- Apply to Pathways Internship programs
- Start: Internship while enrolled in college, convert after graduation
- Cost: Zero (government pays you during internship)
- Timeline: 1-2 years in internship, then permanent position

**Compensation During Internship**:
- GS-2/3: $30K-$38K annually
- GS-4/5: $38K-$48K annually
- Qualifies for federal benefits: health insurance (government pays 70-75%), TSP (Thrift Savings Plan, 5% match = free money), paid leave, job security

**Post-Internship (Permanent Position) Year-1 Salary**:
- GS-5/6: $45K-$55K (typical conversion entry level)
- Full federal benefits package

**Year-5 Salary**:
- GS-7/9: $60K-$85K+ (with normal progression + any special pay)
- Same benefits: insurances, TSP, pension, job security

**Career Ceiling Without Degree**: GS-13+ is typically degree-required. With degree later, career ceiling extends to $150K-$180K+ (senior manager, executive roles).

**Upgrade Paths**: Continue federal service (non-college path limited to ~GS-11-12 ceiling), OR pursue degree while employed (agencies often support this), then advance to senior management. Federal Student Loan Forgiveness (PSLF) = free loan forgiveness after 10 years of public service.

**Demand Trajectory**: Stable. Government hiring varies by administration but always present. Security clearance role shortage ongoing. AI/automation risk: LOW for 10-year horizon (federal processes still heavily manual).

**The Hidden Version / The "Money Glitch"**: Pathways + Security Clearance Path. Apply to Pathways in Defense/Intelligence agencies (DoD, NSA, DIA, etc.). Internship includes security clearance processing (which takes time but doesn't cost you). Post-internship, you're a cleared federal employee. This clearance makes you dramatically valuable in private sector DoD contracting. If you ever leave federal service, you enter the contractor market at $90K-$130K with cleared credentials. Alternatively, stay federal and reach $100K-$140K (SES level) in 10-15 years. This is the realistic government wealth path: not getting rich fast, but building sustained income + unbeatable job security + pension.

**Insider Notes**:
- Pathways is highly competitive (selective agencies) but exists
- Federal benefits are genuinely exceptional: FEHB covers most healthcare, TSP has outstanding Roth options, pension is defined-benefit (rare now), paid parental leave, 13-26 vacation days annually
- GS-7 in LCOL areas is comfortable $55K-$65K equivalent in HCOL regions
- 10-year Public Service Loan Forgiveness for student loans (if you take loans during college, they can be forgiven)

### Military Cyber (Air Force Reserve/National Guard)

**The Role**: Cybersecurity, IT security, network defense. Air Force Reserve or National Guard duty.

**Training Path**:
- Eligibility: U.S. Citizen, pass security check, physical fitness test
- Enlistment as Cyber Operator or related specialty
- Training: 6+ months active duty for initial training
- Cost: ZERO (military pays you, provides housing, food, uniform)
- Certifications: Military provided (Security+, sometimes others)

**Year-1 Compensation**:
- Base pay: $30K-$40K annually (E-2/E-3 rank)
- Housing allowance: $1,000-$1,800/month (if activated/in field)
- Meals provided: ~$400/month value
- Healthcare: TRICARE (cost: $0-$50/month for excellent coverage)
- Educational benefits: GI Bill (cover 100% of in-state college tuition)
- Effective value: ~$55K-$65K total value

**Year-5 Compensation (If Continued)**:
- Base pay: $45K-$60K (E-4/E-5 rank)
- Same benefits plus retirement eligibility starts at 20 years (pension for life)
- Effective value: $75K-$95K

**Career Ceiling**: If you stay 20 years, military retirement pension (50-70% of base pay) starts for life. If you exit after 4-5 years, you're a cleared cyber professional and enter contracting/federal civilian market at $90K-$130K.

**Upgrade Paths**: Pursue officer commission (requires degree or service academy), or transition to civilian federal/contractor cyber roles where your military credentials command premium pay.

**Demand Trajectory**: Growing. Military urgently needs cyber talent. Extremely secure employment. AI/automation risk: VERY LOW (cyber warfare is human-driven, at least for 10+ years).

**The "Money Glitch" Reality**: The realistic wealth play here is not "get rich in military," it's "government pays you to get trained and cleared, then exit into private sector at premium rates." Military Cyber Operator with Secret clearance + 4 years experience → DoD contractor Security Analyst at $100K-$130K. Or, stay military/federal 20 years → pension at age 40-45 + continue working → earn $60K+ pension + new salary = $120K+ lifetime combined income with job security. This is boring but mathematically sound wealth-building.

**Insider Notes**:
- Cyber Reserve/Guard is NOT the same as active duty; it's typically one weekend/month + 2 weeks annual training
- Pay is lower than active duty but compatible with civilian job holding
- This path requires commitment (years of service) but outputs real, protected career path

## Part 5: Sales & Business Development

### Outside Sales / Enterprise Account Executive

**The Role**: Sell B2B software, services, or products to enterprise customers. Larger deal sizes, longer sales cycles, high commission potential.

**Training Path**:
- Formal training: NONE required. Many companies hire college-grad equivalent without degree.
- Self-study: Sales methodology books, online courses (optional)
- Cost: $0-$2K (optional training courses)
- Timeline: Can start with entry-level sales role immediately
- Certifications: Salesforce Administrator cert ($200) is helpful but not required

**Year-1 Salary**:
- Base: $40K-$60K + commission
- Commission: $20K-$50K (highly variable; $10K realistic floor if you work)
- Total potential: $60K-$110K (but floor is closer to $50K-$70K realistically)
- HCOL: Higher commission targets but more competition
- LCOL: Lower commission targets but same % structure

**Year-5 Salary**:
- Base: $60K-$100K
- Commission: $50K-$150K+ (experienced reps hitting quota consistently)
- Total potential: $110K-$250K+
- Realistic floor at year 5 (if decent at job): $100K-$140K

**Career Ceiling**: Unlimited. Top enterprise account executives make $200K-$500K+ (base + commission). Many transition to sales management ($150K-$300K) or leadership roles.

**Upgrade Paths**: Move to larger companies (higher deal sizes = higher commissions), specialize in high-ticket B2B (software, commercial real estate, business services) where deals hit $100K-$1M+, transition to sales management/VP roles.

**Demand Trajectory**: Stable/Growing. Every company sells something. High-paying roles are competitive but exist at scale. AI/automation risk: MODERATE (demo automation tools emerging, but complex sales still human-driven).

**The Hidden Version**: Sales Engineer. Blend of sales + technical knowledge. Software companies need reps who can speak to technical implementation. Pays 15-25% more than pure sales, narrower field = less competition. Requires technical knowledge (self-taught or prior tech job). Year-5 realistic: $120K-$180K.

**Realistic Assessment**: Year-1 in sales is lottery-adjacent. You can earn $70K-$120K if you're natural/driven, or $40K-$60K if you're not. By year 5, if you've stayed and improved, $100K-$180K is reasonable. If you've bounced between companies (learning plays), $130K-$200K is realistic.

## Part 8: Red Flags & Hype Debunking

### The 12-Week Coding Bootcamp Myth

**The Claim**: "Learn to code in 12 weeks and get a $120K job."

**The Reality**:
- Bootcamps teach fundamentals. You're not job-ready after 12 weeks.
- Job outcomes vary wildly: 40-70% of graduates get tech jobs (not stated in marketing)
- Starting salary: $60K-$75K median (not $120K)
- Time to $120K: 3-5 years with deliberate skill building, not 12 weeks
- Cost: $12K-$20K (not stated as significant debt)

**The Verdict**: Bootcamps work IF you're committed, IF you do the project work post-bootcamp, IF you network aggressively. But the 12-week timeline to $120K is false. Realistic: 12 weeks → $55K junior role → 3-5 years to $120K.

---

### The "Passive Income" Freelance Path

**The Claim**: "Build a freelance design business and earn $10K/month passive income."

**The Reality**:
- There's no "passive" in freelancing. You're trading time for money.
- Building to $10K/month takes 2-4 years for most people
- Client acquisition is continuous work (there is no "set it and forget it")
- Income is volatile; feast/famine cycles are real
- Burnout is high (70+ hour weeks to reach high income)

**The Verdict**: Freelancing is viable for $60K-$100K+ earning but not passive. Realistic: 1 year at $30K-$50K, 3 years at $80K-$120K, 5+ years at $100K-$180K (if good and hustling). It's entrepreneurship, not passive income.

---

### The "Learn Content Creation and Make $50K/Month"

**The Claim**: "Start a YouTube channel / TikTok and earn $50K/month in 6 months."

**The Reality**:
- Median YouTube creator: $0 earnings in first 6 months (algorithm is brutal)
- Top 1% of creators make the money; bottom 99% make $0-$500/month
- Building to 100K followers: 1-3 years minimum of consistent content
- Building to monetizable income: 2-5 years minimum
- Income is from ads (pennies per view) unless you have products

**The Verdict**: Content creation is NOT a reliable path to $60K+ in 5 years for most people. Viable for maybe 2-3% of people with timing, talent, and luck. Not recommended as your plan to hit $60K in 5 years. It's a bet, not a career.

## Conclusion: The Real Non-Degree Path to $60K+

### What Works

1. **Skilled Trades** (Electrician, Plumber, HVAC): Reliable, growing, real $75K-$100K+ by year 5. Best for people comfortable with physical work, problem-solving, hands-on learning.

2. **Tech Certs + Experience** (Security+, AWS, Network+): Realistic $85K-$130K by year 5. Best for people who like tech, can self-study, can get first job quickly.

3. **Healthcare (ADN Nursing, Respiratory Therapy)**: Reliable $80K-$105K by year 5. Best for people in healthcare field, good for job security and specialization paths.

4. **Federal/Government Career**: Slower burn but safest wealth-building ($60K-$85K by year 5, but exceptional benefits and pension). Best for people who value security over speed.

5. **Sales** (Enterprise SaaS, Commercial Real Estate): Highest ceiling ($100K-$250K+) but highest variance (year 1 is risky, $40K-$70K possible). Best for people competitive, driven, handle rejection well.

### What's Overhyped

- "Learn to code in 12 weeks and make $120K" - False
- Freelance design as quick path to $100K - False (takes 3-5 years)
- Content creation as reliable income - False (2-3% succeed)
- Any "passive income" path in first 5 years - False

### The Realistic Plan

Pick a path that aligns with your strengths:
- **Physical/hands-on**: Skilled trade (electrician, plumber, HVAC) - 4-5 years to $75K+
- **Tech-minded**: Start with CompTIA A+ or AWS Practitioner, land $45K-$55K job, study for Security+/Network+/Solutions Architect over 18 months while working, reach $90K-$130K by year 5
- **Healthcare**: ADN nursing, 2 years school (low cost), working RN by year 3 at $75K+, reach $85K-$110K by year 5
- **Risk-tolerant**: Sales (SaaS, real estate), $50K-$80K year 1 (variable), $100K-$150K by year 5 if you're good
- **Security-focused**: Federal Pathways internship, 2-3 year path to $60K+ with pension/benefits/job security

The key difference: These paths are real, achievable, and don't require degree debt. They require choosing the right path for your personality, starting on it quickly, and being disciplined for 3-5 years. Most people succeed more by this framework than by college + $37K debt + similar salary.
