## The ROI Trap: Expensive Programs with Poor Outcomes

These are programs at schools costing $30K+ in net price where first-year earnings are below $20K — a critical red flag for education ROI.

| School (Net Price) | Program | 1yr Earnings |
|---------------------|---------|-------------|

| NYU ($37,050) | Design & Applied Arts (MA) | $14,251 |

| NYU ($37,050) | Film/Video Arts (MA) | $15,801 |
| U Miami ($37,244) | Drama/Theatre (BA) | $16,561 |
| Syracuse ($38,793) | Anthropology (BA) | $16,844 |
| Howard ($50,539) | Entertainment Mgmt (BA) | $17,095 |
| Tufts ($39,998) | Fine/Studio Arts (BA) | $17,154 |

**Critical Warning:** These programs cost $30-50K per year in net price but produce graduates earning $13-17K in their first year. At a $37K/year net price over 4 years ($148K total), a Music BA from USC producing $13K in first-year earnings has a debt-to-income ratio above 11x — a catastrophic investment by any financial metric. This does not mean these fields are worthless, but funding them at these price points is financially destructive.

**The rule:** If a student's passion is in these fields, the financial advice is clear — pursue them at a school where the net price is under $10K/year (community college, in-state public, or full scholarship at a private). The education is similar; the financial outcome is dramatically different.

## Decision Frameworks

### Framework 1: School Selection by Financial Outcome

When a student has multiple acceptances:
1. Look up each school's 10-year median earnings in Scorecard data
2. Calculate the value ratio: 10yr earnings / net price
3. Compare program-specific earnings for the intended major, not just school-wide medians
4. A school with 2x the value ratio of another will deliver a dramatically better financial outcome

### Framework 2: Major Selection Within a School

Same school, different major = vastly different outcomes:
- Engineering and CS programs at any top-50 school → $80-120K first-year earnings (masters)
- Business/finance programs at top schools → $100-220K (MBA/masters)
- Fine arts, music, anthropology at the same schools → $13-25K first-year earnings
- The school's overall ranking is less important than the program-specific earnings data

### Framework 3: The Wharton Paradox

UPenn's Wharton programs (Finance: $216K, Business Admin: $210K, Entrepreneurship: $220K) show that business programs at elite schools have exceptional outcomes. But UPenn's humanities programs have dramatically lower outcomes. The school-wide median of $111K is an average that masks these extremes. Always look at program-level data, not school-level data.
