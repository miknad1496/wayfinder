# Wayfinder General Brain — Condensed Career Intelligence

## Core Philosophy
Wayfinder gives honest, data-backed career advice. We don't sugarcoat — if a major has poor ROI, we say so while offering alternatives. We believe in optionality: the best career decisions keep doors open. We're anti-prestige, pro-outcome.

## Key Data Points
- Median US salary: ~$59K. Bachelor's holders: ~$75K. Master's: ~$81K. Doctoral: ~$100K+.
- Fastest-growing fields (BLS 2032): Healthcare (nurse practitioners +38%, data scientists +35%, information security analysts +32%).
- Most automation-resistant careers: Healthcare, skilled trades, creative strategy, complex problem-solving roles.
- Psychology BA median: $43.8K without grad school. CS BA median: $97K. Nursing BSN: $81.2K.
- Skilled trades (electricians, plumbers) earn $60-90K with zero student debt and 2-year paths.
- Bootcamp grads in software average $65-85K starting; ROI beats many 4-year degrees for career changers.
- Federal jobs: 2M+ positions, PSLF loan forgiveness, 4.4% average raises, strong benefits. USAJobs.gov is the portal.

## Decision Frameworks
- **Major Selection**: Consider earnings trajectory (not starting salary), optionality (what doors does it open?), and personal fit. STEM majors have higher floors but liberal arts + skills can match ceilings.
- **Grad School**: Only if the career REQUIRES it or ROI is clear. Med/law/MBA have strong ROI at top programs. Master's in education or humanities rarely pays off financially.
- **Career Pivots**: Most successful pivots leverage 60%+ of existing skills. Best windows: 2-4 years experience (low cost to switch) or 8-10 years (enough leverage for lateral moves).
- **Job Offers**: Evaluate total compensation (base + benefits + equity + growth), not just salary. A $70K offer with great mentorship can beat $90K at a dead-end.

## Audience Notes
- **High school undecided**: Focus on exploration, not commitment. Community college and gap years are valid. Avoid $200K debt without a plan.
- **First-generation students**: Navigate hidden curriculum. Imposter syndrome is normal. Seek mentors, not just advisors. Financial aid offices are underutilized.
- **Career changers (30s+)**: Your experience IS an asset. Transferable skills matter more than new degrees. Network > resume at this stage.

## Contrarian Insights
- "Follow your passion" is often bad advice. Better: find what you're good at that the market values.
- Unpaid internships usually aren't worth it — paid ones and real projects matter more.
- GPA matters less than most think after first job. Skills, portfolio, and network dominate.
- The "skills gap" is largely a hiring-practices gap. Most jobs train on the job if you have the foundation.
- Remote work has permanently expanded geographic arbitrage — high-skill, low-cost-of-living is a real strategy.
