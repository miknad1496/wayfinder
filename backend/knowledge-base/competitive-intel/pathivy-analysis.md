# Competitive Intelligence: PathIvy

> Source: Email to 10th grade families, March 2026
> Founder: Vicky (Vandana) Mehta — MBA Stanford GSB, Ex-Microsoft PM
> Status: Discontinued by our user (Dan) due to poor experience

---

## WHAT PATHIVY DOES
Traditional college admissions consulting. Weekly counselor meetings, mandatory bootcamps (3-hour Sunday sessions), webinars, essay workshops. Covers the full 11th-12th grade application cycle.

## THEIR MODEL
- **Format**: One-size-fits-all webinars + bootcamps + weekly 1:1 counselor meetings
- **Schedule**: Every Sunday, 4-5pm PT (webinars) or 4-7pm PT (bootcamps)
- **Pricing**: Likely $3,000-$8,000+ per year (typical for this tier of consulting)
- **Approach**: Process-driven checklist (develop theme → build list → draft essays → submit)
- **Tone**: Authoritarian ("non-negotiable for success", "mandatory", "must be done")

## THEIR CONTENT CALENDAR (2026)
Covers: international applications, SAT/ACT prep, AP/IB prep, college essay brainstorming, activity lists, LinkedIn profiles, UC applications, major strategy (CS, pre-med, business), passion projects, interviews, research, competitions, PSAT/merit scholarships, UCAS (UK)

## WHAT'S WRONG WITH THIS MODEL (User Feedback)

### Problem 1: Students are BORED and DISENGAGED
- Webinars are low-energy, not interesting or insightful
- Students don't want to attend — feels like homework, not help
- "Factory-like method of throwing garbage at kids"
- Content sounds impressive to PARENTS (who pay) but fails STUDENTS (who need it)

### Problem 2: One-Size-Fits-All
- Same webinar for every student regardless of their specific needs
- A student targeting MIT CS gets the same "How to Prepare for SAT/ACT" webinar as someone targeting Alabama
- No personalization based on student's actual profile, gaps, or goals

### Problem 3: Lecture Format ≠ Learning
- 1-hour webinars on "College Application Components" — what does that even teach?
- 3-hour bootcamps on Sundays — who has that attention span?
- Passive consumption (watching/listening) vs active engagement (doing/discovering)

### Problem 4: Process Over Substance
- Heavy on logistics (timelines, checklists, "submit 2 weeks before deadline")
- Light on the deep strategic thinking that actually moves outcomes
- "Develop a clear application theme" — but HOW? The webinar tells you to do it, not how to do it

### Problem 5: Credential Signaling Over Delivery
- Stanford MBA, Ex-Microsoft PM sounds impressive
- But the actual product is generic webinars that could be found on YouTube
- Parents buy the credentials, students suffer the content
