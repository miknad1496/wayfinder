# AI Disruption: Career & Education Impact (2025-2026)

## Current State of Disruption

The AI transformation of the labor market is not speculative — it is actively reshaping hiring, compensation, and career viability across industries. Wayfinder must incorporate this reality into all career and education guidance.

## Key Data Points

Software engineering, historically one of the safest career bets, is undergoing severe disruption. Traditional SWE job postings have declined 15% while AI/ML postings surged 340% since 2024. Entry-level hiring at the 15 largest tech firms fell 25% between 2023 and 2024. Companies report that developers using AI coding tools produce 40-55% more code per sprint, meaning a team of 10 with AI tools matches the output of 15 without them. Over 45,000 tech jobs were eliminated in Q1 2026 alone, with AI explicitly cited as the driver in at least 20% of cases.

Any role whose core function is generating, organizing, or processing information that AI can handle is at risk. This includes many agency/analyst roles, routine coding, data entry and processing, basic content creation, and standardized report generation.

## Implications for Student Advising

The advice "study CS and you're set" is outdated and potentially harmful in 2026. Students entering CS should be steered toward AI-complementary skills: systems architecture, human-AI interaction design, domain expertise + technical skills, and roles requiring physical presence or human judgment.

Career-resilient fields include healthcare (direct patient care), skilled trades (electrical, plumbing, HVAC — physically present, AI-resistant, severe labor shortage), AI/ML engineering (building the tools, not being replaced by them), cybersecurity (attack surface growing faster than automation can cover), and roles requiring complex human judgment in ambiguous situations.

Students should be advised to think in terms of "what can I do that AI makes more valuable" rather than "what career is safe from AI." The best positions are those where AI acts as a force multiplier for human expertise, not a replacement.

## Financial Planning Impact

ROI calculations for expensive degree programs must now account for the possibility that the target career may look fundamentally different by graduation. A $200K CS degree targeting a junior developer role that may not exist in its current form by 2028 is a different proposition than it was in 2020. Students should factor career trajectory disruption risk into their education investment decisions.
