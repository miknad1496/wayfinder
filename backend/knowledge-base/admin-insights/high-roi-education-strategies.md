# High-ROI Education Strategies Wayfinder Should Proactively Surface

## Philosophy

Wayfinder should not default to the assumption that a traditional four-year residential college experience is the right path for every student. The highest-ROI strategies often involve creative sequencing of educational experiences that minimize debt while maximizing credential value and career positioning.

## Running Start / Dual Enrollment to Transfer

Running Start (Washington state) and similar dual enrollment programs across the country allow high school juniors and seniors to take college courses — often at zero tuition cost. In Washington, students can earn an Associate's degree by high school graduation, then apply to the University of Washington or other four-year schools as a first-year student (not a transfer), which qualifies them for more generous first-year financial aid packages.

The financial math: two years of free college courses during high school + two years at a state university = bachelor's degree for roughly half the cost of four years at the university. Students who use this strategy can save $40-60K compared to the traditional path.

Variants exist in most states: dual enrollment (Florida, Texas, many others), College Credit Plus (Ohio), Post-Secondary Enrollment Options (Minnesota, Colorado), and similar programs. Advisors should identify the local equivalent for each student's state.

Key strategic note: some elite private schools may view Running Start or dual enrollment credits differently than AP courses. For students targeting top-20 schools, AP courses may carry more admissions weight. For students targeting state flagships or prioritizing cost savings, Running Start is almost always the better financial decision.

## Community College to Guaranteed Transfer

California's TAG (Transfer Admission Guarantee) is the gold standard: complete the required coursework at a California CC with the minimum GPA and admission to specific UC campuses is guaranteed. Six UC campuses participate (Davis, Irvine, Merced, Riverside, Santa Barbara, Santa Cruz).

Similar articulation agreements exist in Texas (transfer compacts), Virginia (guaranteed admission agreements with state schools), and many other states. The advisor should always check the student's state-specific transfer pathways.

Financial advantage: CC at $3,500-5,000/year for two years + state flagship for two years = $60-80K total vs $100-140K for four years at the flagship. Identical diploma. Career outcomes within 5% by year five.

## Trades and Apprenticeships

For students who are not academically inclined, not sure what they want to study, or highly cost-conscious, skilled trades offer exceptional ROI. Electricians, plumbers, HVAC technicians, and welders earn $50-80K median with experienced professionals clearing $100K+. Apprenticeships pay $15-25/hour during training. Zero student debt. Severe labor shortage means near-guaranteed employment. These roles are AI-resistant because they require physical presence and manual skill.

The business ownership path amplifies returns further: licensed tradespeople who start their own shops can earn $150K+.

## Credential Stacking

For some career paths, a targeted stack of industry certifications + associate degree + work experience outperforms a bachelor's degree at a fraction of the cost. Examples: CompTIA certifications + community college IT courses for cybersecurity roles; coding bootcamp + portfolio projects for web development; CNA + associates in nursing for RN pathway.

This approach is particularly strong for students who learn better by doing, who need to earn income quickly, or whose target career doesn't have a hard degree requirement.
